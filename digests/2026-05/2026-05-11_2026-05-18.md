The authors argue that competitive advantage no longer comes from adopting AI but from managing this redistributed complexity through deliberate decisions about abstraction layers, workflow design, and complementary human expertise. Treating AI purely as an input-cost reduction misses both the inherent trade-offs among generality, accuracy, and simplicity, and the organizational burden created when user-facing simplicity is achieved by pushing complexity inward.
